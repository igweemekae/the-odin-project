White Soup

Description

White soup is a delectable and aromatic palm nut soup that hails from the southern part of Nigeria, celebrated for its rich,
earthy flavors derived from fresh palm nut extract and a blend of traditional spices, often paired with fresh seafood or assorted meats.

Ingredients

  • Fresh palm nuts or palm nut cream concentrate
  • Assorted meats (such as beef, goat meat, and cow tripe)
  • Stockfish
  • Crayfish (ground)
  • Banga spices (pre-mixed spices for Banga soup, often containing African nutmeg and rohojie)
  • Ataiko and Irugeje (optional Banga spices commonly found in southern Nigerian markets)
  • Ground crayfish (2 tablespoons)
  • Fresh peppers (such as Scotch Bonnet or Habanero)
  • Onions
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water
  • Seasoning cubes

Steps to Prepare White soup

Prepare the Palm Nut Extract:
If using fresh palm nuts, boil them until tender, then pound in a mortar to extract the juice. Strain the extract into a pot.
If using palm nut cream concentrate, dilute it with water according to package instructions.

Cook the Meats:
In a large pot, add the assorted meats, stockfish, and dry fish. Season with salt, seasoning cubes, and chopped fresh peppers.
Add enough water to cover the meat and cook until the meats are tender.

Prepare the Soup Base:
Pour the palm nut extract into a large pot and bring to a simmer.
Add the cooked meats and stockfish along with some of the broth from cooking the meats.

Enhance with Spices:
Incorporate ground crayfish, Banga spices, ataiko, and irugeje (if using) into the simmering pot.
Add fresh pepper and more seasoning cubes if needed, adjusting to taste.

Add Fish:
Gently add fresh or dried fish to the pot, taking care not to break it apart as it cooks.

Introduce Vegetables:
After the fish has cooked, add a handful of chopped bitter leaf, scent leaf, or basil for flavor and color.
Simmer:
Allow the soup to cook on low heat for an additional 10-15 minutes, letting all the flavors meld and the soup thicken slightly.
Serve:
Once the Banga soup reaches your desired consistency and taste, remove from heat and serve hot. It pairs beautifully with starches like pounded yam, fufu, or white rice.
